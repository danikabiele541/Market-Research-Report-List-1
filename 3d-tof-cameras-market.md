<p><p>The 3D Time-of-Flight (ToF) camera market is primarily divided into two types: CMOS and CCD ToF cameras. CMOS Time-of-Flight cameras utilize complementary metal-oxide-semiconductor technology, offering advantages like lower power consumption, compact size, and faster readout speeds, making them suitable for mobile and real-time applications. In contrast, CCD Time-of-Flight cameras utilize charge-coupled device technology, which typically provides higher image quality and lower noise levels, making them advantageous in industrial and scientific settings where precision is critical.</p></p>
