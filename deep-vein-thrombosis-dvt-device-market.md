<p><p>The Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT) device market primarily comprises venous stents and intermittent pneumatic compression devices. Venous stents are utilized to maintain blood flow in affected veins, preventing the recurrence of clots and alleviating symptoms. Intermittent pneumatic compression devices are designed to promote blood circulation through rhythmic inflation and deflation of cuffs, effectively reducing the risk of clot formation. Both device types aim to improve patient outcomes and enhance preventive measures against DVT, particularly in high-risk populations.</p></p>
