<p><p>The automotive natural gas vehicle (NGV) market primarily consists of two types: Compressed Natural Gas (CNG) vehicles and Liquefied Natural Gas (LNG) vehicles. CNG vehicles utilize compressed natural gas stored at high pressure, making them suitable for urban and light-duty applications due to their lower emissions and refueling convenience. In contrast, LNG vehicles use liquefied natural gas, which is more energy-dense and ideal for heavy-duty applications like long-haul trucking. Both types contribute to reduced greenhouse gas emissions and offer a cleaner alternative to gasoline and diesel.</p></p>
