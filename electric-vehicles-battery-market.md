<p><p>The electric vehicle (EV) battery market primarily consists of three types: lithium-ion (Li-ion), nickel-metal hydride (Ni-MH), and other battery technologies. Lithium-ion batteries dominate due to their high energy density, lightweight, and decreasing cost, making them ideal for EVs. Ni-MH batteries, once popular in hybrid vehicles, offer good lifespan but lower energy density compared to Li-ion. Other battery technologies, such as solid-state and sodium-ion, are emerging, promising improvements in safety, efficiency, and sustainability for future EV applications.</p></p>
