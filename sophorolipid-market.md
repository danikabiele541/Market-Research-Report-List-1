<p><p>The sophorolipid market is primarily divided into two types: lactonic and acidic sophorolipids. Lactonic sophorolipids are characterized by their lactone ring structure, which enhances their emulsifying and foaming properties, making them suitable for personal care and cosmetic applications. In contrast, acidic sophorolipids lack this structural feature, providing stronger surfactant qualities primarily utilized in industrial applications, including cleaning products. Both types are derived from renewable resources, contributing to their appeal in sustainable product formulations.</p></p>
