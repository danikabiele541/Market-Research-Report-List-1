<p><ul><li>Immune Vaccine</li><li>Therapy Vaccine</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The tuberculosis vaccination market can be categorized into two main types: immune vaccines and therapy vaccines. Immune vaccines are designed to prevent tuberculosis infection by stimulating the body’s immune response against Mycobacterium tuberculosis, thereby providing protective immunity. In contrast, therapy vaccines target individuals already infected, aiming to enhance the immune response to help control the disease and prevent progression to active tuberculosis. Both types play crucial roles in tuberculosis prevention and management strategies.</p></p>
