<p><p>Copper electroplating for IC substrates involves depositing a copper layer onto a substrate to enhance electrical conductivity and provide structural integrity. The market is primarily segmented into acid plating and alkaline plating types. Acid plating utilizes sulfuric acid to achieve a smooth finish and improved adhesion, ensuring uniform deposition. In contrast, alkaline plating employs a sodium hydroxide solution, resulting in higher deposition rates and better coverage in complex geometries, making both methods essential for advanced semiconductor manufacturing.</p></p>
