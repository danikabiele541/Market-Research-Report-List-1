<p><p>The automotive backup camera lens market primarily features two types: CCD and CMOS cameras. CCD cameras are known for their high image quality, especially in low-light conditions, making them ideal for premium vehicles. They are more expensive and consume more power. CMOS cameras, on the other hand, are cost-effective, energy-efficient, and widely used in mass-market vehicles. While CMOS cameras have improved in quality, they may still lag behind CCD in low-light performance. Both types enhance safety by providing clear rearview images.</p></p>
